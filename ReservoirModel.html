Jupyter notebook solution for the mass of carbon in the major reservoirs over Earth's history¶

1). The objective of our study is to model the extraction of carbon from the atmosphere and/or mantle into the continental crust over Earth's history (the last 4.4 Ga).

import numpy as np

    return np.arange(0,tp,1e6)

tp = 4.4e9
t = time(tp)
billion_years = 1e9
constFactor = 1e8

2). We do not consider carbon in the Earth's core, assuming insignificant transfer of carbon between the core and mantle over the last 4.4 Ga.

3). The mantle and continental crust reservoirs are the only interacting reservoirs that contain large masses of carbon at the present time. The masses of carbon in the oceans and atmosphere reservoirs are many orders of magnitude smaller.

4). The atmosphere of Venus contains a large mass of carbon, the atmosphere of the Earth may have contained a large mass of carbon in the past.

5). We specify the present mass of carbon in the continental crust ${^c}M_{ccp}$. We assume that the carbon in calcium carbonates is dominant and neglect other forms of carbon in the continental crust.

6). We assume the carbon in the continental crust has been produced by the Urey reaction. Atmospheric $CO_2$ combines with calcium silicates to form calcium carbonates.

7). We consider two origins for the carbon in the continental crust:

  • the direct extraction of primordial $CO_2$ from the atmosphere and
  • the extraction of $CO_2$ in the atmosphere that entered the atmosphere from the mantle by surface volcanism.

    We specify the mass of carbon that entered the continental crust that was in the primordial atmosphere ${^cM}_{a-cc}$. The mass of carbon that originated in the mantle is given by \begin{equation} {^cM}_{m-cc} = {^cM}_{ccp} - {^cM}_{a-cc} \tag{1} \end{equation}

8). We specify the age of the Earth $t_1$ when plate tectonics and the existence of continents and oceans led to the activation of the Urey reaction and the volcanic transfer of carbon from the mantle to the surface of the Earth.

9). We assume the flux of carbon from the mantle to the continental crust has been constant from time $t_1$ to the present $t_p = 4.4$ Gyrs. This flux is given by

\begin{equation} {^cF}_{m-cc} = \frac{{^cM}_{m-cc}}{(t_p - t_1)} \tag{2} \end{equation}

10). We specify the time span for the extraction of carbon from the atmosphere to the continental crust at a high rate $\tau_{a-cc}$.

11). During the period $t_1 < t < t_1 + \tau_{a-cc}$ the flux rate of carbon from the primordial atmosphere to the continental crust is given by

\begin{equation} {^cF}_{a-cc} = \frac{{^cM}_{a-cc}}{\tau_{a-cc}} \tag{3} \end{equation}

12). During the period $t_1 < t < t_1 + \tau_{a-cc}$ the flux rate ${^cF}_{a-cc}$ is controlled by the availability of carbon silicates to interact with atmospheric $CO_2$ to form calcium carbonates via the Urey reaction. The time interval $\tau_{a-cc}$ is a measure of this rate, its value is not well constrained.

13). During the period $t_1 < t < t_1 + \tau_{a-cc}$ the mass of carbon in the atmosphere satisfies the equation

\begin{equation} \frac{d\,{^cM}_a}{dt} = -\frac{{^cM}_{a-cc}}{\tau_{a-cc}} \tag{4} \end{equation}

The solution to this equation that satisfies the conditions ${^cM}_a = 0$ at $t=t_1 + \tau_{a-cc}$ is

\begin{equation} {^cM}_a = {^cM}_{a-cc} \,\Big(1 -\frac{t-t_1}{\tau_{a-cc}}\Big) \tag{5} \end{equation}

The mass of atmospheric carbon at $t=t_1 + \tau_{a-cc}$ is not zero but is very small compared to the initial value ${^cM}_{a-cc}$ as we will show below.

def evolution_of_carbon_atmosphere_t1_to_t1_tau_acc(time, t1, tau_acc, M_acc_0):
    t = np.array([ele for ele in time if t1 < ele and ele <= t1 + tau_acc])
    M_atm = M_acc_0 * (-t/tau_acc + (t1+tau_acc)/tau_acc)
    return M_atm

14). During the time period $t_1 < t < t_1 + \tau_{a-cc}$ the mass of carbon in the continental crust satisfies the equation

\begin{equation} \frac{d\,{^cM}_{cc}}{dt} = \frac{{^cM}_{a-cc}}{\tau_{a-cc}} + \frac{{^cM}_{m-cc}}{t_p - t_1} \tag{6} \end{equation}

The solution of this equation that satisfies the condition ${^cM}_{cc} = 0$ at $t = t_1$ is

\begin{equation} {^cM}_{cc} = \Big(\frac{{^cM}_{a-cc}}{\tau_{a-cc}} + \frac{{^cM}_{m-cc}}{t_p - t_1}\Big)(t - t_1) \tag{7} \end{equation}

At the end of this period $t = t_1 + \tau_{a-cc}$, the mass of the carbon in the continental crust is

\begin{equation} {^cM}_{cc}(t=t_1 + \tau_{a-cc}) = {^cM}_{a-cc} + {^cM}_{m-cc}\big(\frac{\tau_{a-cc}}{t_p - t_1}\big) \tag{8} \end{equation}

def evolution_of_carbon_cc_t1_to_t1_tau_acc(time, t1, tp, tau_acc, M_acc, M_ccp):
    t = np.array([ele for ele in time if t1 < ele and ele <= t1 + tau_acc])
    M_mcc = M_ccp - M_acc
    M_cc = (M_acc/tau_acc + M_mcc/(tp-t1)) * (t-t1)
    return M_cc

15). During the time period $t_1 + \tau_{a-cc} < t < t_p$ the mass of carbon in the continental crust satisfies the equation

\begin{equation} \frac{d\,{^cM}_{cc}}{dt} = \frac{{^cM}_{m-cc}}{t_p - t_1} \tag{9} \end{equation}

and the mass of carbon in the continental crust reservoir is given by

\begin{equation} {^cM}_{cc} = {^cM}_{a-cc} + {^cM}_{m-cc}\big(\frac{t - t_1}{t_p - t_1}\big) \tag{10} \end{equation}

which gives ${^cM}_{ccp} = {^cM}_{a-cc} + {^cM}_{m-cc}$ as required.

def evolution_of_carbon_cc_t1_tau_acc_to_tp(time, t1, tp, tau_acc, M_acc, M_ccp):
    t = np.array([ele for ele in time if t1 + tau_acc < ele and ele <= tp])
    M_mcc = M_ccp - M_acc
    M_cc = M_acc + M_mcc * ((t - t1)/(tp-t1))
    return M_cc

16). During the entire time period $t_1 < t < t_p$ the mass of carbon in the mantle satisfies the equation

\begin{equation} \frac{d\,{^cM}_{m}}{dt} = -\frac{{^cM}_{m-cc}}{t_p - t_1} \tag{11} \end{equation}

and the mass of carbon in the mantle is given by

\begin{equation} {^cM}_{m} = {^cM}_{mp} - {^cM}_{m-cc}\big(\frac{t - t_1}{t_p - t_1}\big) \tag{12} \end{equation}

where ${^cM}_{mp}$ the present mass of carbon in the mantle is specified.

def evolution_of_carbon_mantle_t1_to_tp(time, t1, tp, M_mp, M_acc, M_ccp):
    t = np.array([ele for ele in time if t1 < ele and ele <= tp])
    M_mcc = M_ccp - M_acc
    M_m =  M_mp - M_mcc * (t-t1)/(tp-t1)
    return M_m

Prescribed values and ranges of values¶

  1. Present mass of carbon in the continental crust
    ${^cM}_{ccp} = 4 \times 10^7$ Gt   $(2-10)$

  2. Mass of carbon that entered the continental crust from the primordial atmosphere
    ${^cM}_{a-cc} = 2.1 \times 10^7$ Gt   $(0 - {^cM}_{ccp})$

  3. Age of the Earth when plate tectonics and the existence of continents and oceans led to the activation of Urey reaction
    $t_1 = 1$ Gyr   $(0.5 - 2)$

  4. Time span for the extraction of carbon from the atmosphere to the continental crust
    $\tau_{a-cc} = 1$ Gyr   $(0.5 -2)$

  5. Present mass of carbon in the mantle
    ${^cM}_{mp} = 2 \times 10^8$ Gt   $(1-4)$.

def reservoir_model(time, t1, M_ccp, M_acc, tau_acc, M_mp):
    plt.close('all')
#     plt.rc('text', usetex=True)
    
#     plt.figure(figsize=(8,6))
    fig, (ax1) = plt.subplots(nrows=1,ncols=1,figsize=(14,9), sharex=False)
        
    lw = 4
    fz_label=28
    fz=26
    
    # For time t0 to t1:
    t = np.array([ele for ele in time if ele <= t1])
    M_cc = np.zeros(len(t))
    M_atm_t0 = M_acc #Gt
    M_atm = np.full(len(t), M_acc)
    
    # For time t1 to t1+tau_acc:
    M_atm = np.append(M_atm, evolution_of_carbon_atmosphere_t1_to_t1_tau_acc(time, t1, tau_acc, M_acc))
    M_cc = np.append(M_cc, evolution_of_carbon_cc_t1_to_t1_tau_acc(time, t1, tp, tau_acc, M_acc, M_ccp))
    
    #For time t1 to tp
    M_m_tmp = evolution_of_carbon_mantle_t1_to_tp(time, t1, tp, M_mp, M_acc, M_ccp)
    M_m = np.append(np.full(len(t), M_m_tmp[0]), M_m_tmp)
     
    #For time t1+tau_acc to tp
    t = np.array([ele for ele in time if t1 + tau_acc < ele and ele <= tp])
    M_cc = np.append(M_cc, evolution_of_carbon_cc_t1_tau_acc_to_tp(time, t1, tp, tau_acc, M_acc, M_ccp))
    M_atm = np.append(M_atm, np.zeros(len(t)))
    
    #Plotting:
    M_cm_line = ax1.plot(time/billion_years, M_m/constFactor, linewidth=lw, color='orange')
    plt.annotate('Mantle', xy=(0.05, 0.95), xytext=(time[-1]*0.75/billion_years, M_m[-1]*0.92/constFactor), size=fz)

    M_cc_line = ax1.plot(time/billion_years, M_cc/constFactor, linewidth=lw, color='black')
    plt.annotate('Continental Crust', xy=(0.05, 0.95), xytext=(time[-1]*0.65/billion_years, M_cc[-1]*1.20/constFactor), size=fz)

    M_ca_line = ax1.plot(time/billion_years, M_atm/constFactor, linewidth=lw, color='cyan')
    plt.annotate('Atmosphere', xy=(0.05, 0.95), xytext=(time[-1]*0.70/billion_years,(M_atm[-1] + 0.1e8)/constFactor), size=fz)

    ax1.set_xlabel('Time ($10^9$ years)', size=fz_label)
    ax1.set_ylabel('Mass of Carbon ($10^8$ Gt)', size=fz_label)
#     plt.savefig('ModelM-CC.png', format='png')

    x_axis_ticks = np.arange(0,6,1)
